Sunlight Zone (Epipelagic) — 0 to 200m

The World You Know

Warm. Lit. Teeming with life.
Below this line, the rules change.

You are leaving the familiar behind. Nearly 90% of all marine life exists within this warm, sunlit layer because it is the only zone where photosynthesis is possible. But sunlight is a privilege. As you sink past 200 meters, the plants disappear, the water turns frigid, and the survival mechanics of the entire ocean shift from relying on the sun into relying on the shadows.

Twilight Zone (Mesopelagic) — 200 to 1000m

The Light Dies Here

Only 1% of sunlight reaches this depth.
What lives here makes its own light.

The ocean turns into an endless dusk. You are now entering the staging ground of the greatest daily migration on our planet: millions of creatures rise to the surface at night to feed, and sink back into the twilight by day to hide from predators. To survive total darkness, evolution invented bioluminescence—a living chemical light used to hunt, mate, and deceive.

Abyssal Plain (Abyssopelagic) — 1000 to 6000m

The Desert of the Deep

Pressure here is 600 times what you feel right now.
And still — life finds a way.

You are now in the freezer of the deep. The water is near freezing, and the pressure is crushing—equivalent to an African elephant standing on your thumb. Food is so overwhelmingly scarce that meals arrive merely as 'marine snow' drifting down from dead organisms far above. Predators here, like the Anglerfish, cannot afford to chase prey. They must conserve energy, waiting in total stillness for a meal to wander toward a deceptive, glowing lure.

Hadal Zone (Hadalpelagic) — 6000 to 11,000m

Welcome to the Challenger Deep. You are standing further down from sea-level than the peak of Mount Everest is up in the sky. Down here in the jagged tectonic trenches, life doesn't rely on the sun at all. It sustains itself by converting toxic, boiling chemicals spewing from hydrothermal vents into energy. It is an alien world existing silently on our own planet, proving that where there is water, life will always conquer the impossible.
